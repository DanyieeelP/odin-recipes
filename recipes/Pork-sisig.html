Pork Sisig

This recipe is created by Vanjo Merano in his Panlasang pinoy website Panlasangpinoy.com.

Pork sisig is a famous Filipino dish made with pig's face (Maskara), ears, belly served with sizzling plate

It's perfect for main dishes or Pulutan (snack with drinks)

Ingredients

  • 1 lb. pig ears
  • 1 lb. pig snout
  • 1 lb pork belly
  • 2 pieces onions minced
  • 3 pieces bay leaves
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 4 thumbs ginger
  • 1 quartz water

Sisig Dressing:

  • ½ cup Lady's Choice Mayonaise
  • 2 tablespoons sukang iloko (Iloco)
  • ¼ teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • ¼ cup liver spread
  • 2 limes
  • 1 tablespoon Knorr liquid seasoning

Directions

  1. Combine pig ears, snout, pork belly, ginger, bay leaves, 2 teaspoons salt, and 1 quart of water in a cooking pot.
    Boil for 1 hour.
    Remove from the pot and drain the water.
  2. Combine pig ears, snout, pork belly, ginger, bay leaves, 2 teaspoons salt, and 1 quart of water in a cooking pot.
    Boil for 1 hour. Remove from the pot and drain the water.
  3. Make the dressing by combining all the dressing ingredients. Mix well.
  4. Chop the grilled pig parts into small pieces.
    In a large mixing bowl, combine the chopped pork with onion and the dressing mixture.
    Toss until all the ingredients are well blended.
  5. Transfer to a serving plate. Serve with your favorite drink. Share and enjoy!
