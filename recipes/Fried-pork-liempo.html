Fried Pork Liempo

This recipe is created by Vanjo Merano in his Panlasang pinoy website

Fried pork liempo is a popular Filipino dish made with Pork belly that is seasoned, boiled, then deep fried or air-fried until golden crunchy.

It is commonly served with rice and dipping sauce made with garlic, chili, and onions.

Ingredients

  • 3 lbs pork belly sliced
  • 5 gloves garlic crushed
  • 8 grams Maggi Magic sarap
  • ½ teaspoon ground black pepper
  • ½ cup all-purpose flour
  • 3 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 ½ cups water
  • ½ cup cooking oil

Dipping Sauce ingredients:

  • 5 cloves garlic crushed
  • 1 piece onion chopped
  • 5 pieces Thai chilli pepper chopped
  • 1 teaspoon granulated white sugar
  • ¼ teaspoon ground black pepper
  • ¼ teaspoon salt

Directions

  1. Arrange the sliced pork belly in a pan.
    Season with Maggi Magic Sarap and ground black pepper.
    (3 lbs pork belly, 8 grams Maggi Magic Sarap, ½ teaspoon ground black pepper)
  2. Add the garlic and then pour water into the pan. Boil for 10 minutes.
    (5 cloves garlic, 1 ½ cups water)
  3. Flip the pork over and continue boiling until the water completely evaporates.
  4. Remove the pork. Let it cool down.
  5. Combine all-purpose flour and cornstarch in a bowl. Mix well.
    Dredge the boiled pork belly and shake-off excess flour. Set aside.
    (½ cup all-purpose flour, 3 tablespoons cornstarch)
  6. Heat the oil in a clean pan. Once hot, start to fry the pork belly until it brown.
    Turn it over and do the same thing on the other side.
    (½ cup cooking oil)
  7. Remove from the pan and arrange on a serving plate.
  8. Combine all the dipping sauce ingredients in a bowl. Mix well.
    (5 cloves garlic, 1 piece onion, 5 pieces Thai chili pepper, 1 tablespoon green onion,
    1 teaspoon granulated white sugar, ¼ teaspoon ground black pepper, ¼ teaspoon salt)
  9. Serve the fried pork belly with the dipping sauce and steamed white rice. Share and enjoy!
